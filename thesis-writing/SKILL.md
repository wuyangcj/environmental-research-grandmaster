# Thesis & Journal Paper Writing (论文撰写)

## Overview
This skill transforms raw research into a structured, persuasive academic narrative following the IMRaD (Introduction, Methods, Results, and Discussion) model.

## 1. Manuscript Structure (IMRaD)
- **Abstract**: Objective, Methods, Key Results, Conclusion (max 250 words).
- **Introduction**: Background -> Problem -> Literature Gap -> Research Question.
- **Methods**: Reproducibility is key. Provide exact parameters, equipment, and software versions.
- **Results**: Present data objectively. Use "Top Tier" visualizations.
- **Discussion**: Interpret results, compare with SOTA, and state limitations.

## 2. Argument Construction
- **Claim-Evidence-Warrant**: Every claim must be backed by data (Results) and supported by theory (Literature).
- **Transitions**: Ensure logical flow between paragraphs (e.g., "Building on the results of Section 3.1...").

## 3. Technical Polish
- **Tone**: Formal, objective, and passive/active voice as per journal style.
- **Nomenclature**: Use standard SI units and consistent chemical/biological naming.

## Checklists for "论文"
- [ ] Does the Abstract contain the most important numerical result?
- [ ] Is the "Novelty Statement" clearly articulated in the Introduction?
- [ ] Are all figures referenced in the text (e.g., "As shown in Figure 1...")?
